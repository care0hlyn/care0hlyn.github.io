About:

I'm a front end developer and graduate student at the University of Washington in the Human Centered Design & Engineering program. My focus is on user centered design and international research.

In 2015, I was fortunate to give a tech talk for 'Women Who Code' in Hong Kong on front end development and career goals. As an advocate for women and minorities in tech, I continuously seek ways to do more outreach.

A voracious learner, I am enthusiastic about virtual reality, arduino, 3D printing, minimalistic design, yoga, Bertrand Russell, feminism...and yes, food. Which leads to my secret life as a co-owner of an instant ramen food cart (@nomnommarket) here in sunny Seattle, WA.

My professional interests include UX research (case studies + usability testing), UX design (wireframing + prototyping), data vis and iOS development. Feel free to reach out and check out my CV.
